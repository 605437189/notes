    

# Lucy

Q: in the moive Lucy , what questions are being proposed

A:

    Of course. The movie _Lucy_, directed by Luc Besson, uses its high-concept sci-fi thriller premise to propose a series of deep philosophical and scientific questions. They are more "what if" propositions than questions with answers.

Here are the central questions being proposed:

### 1. What if We Could Access 100% of Our Brain?

This is the core premise and the most famous question the movie poses. It popularizes a common myth (that we only use 10% of our brain) and explores the hypothetical consequences. The film asks:

- What untapped abilities would we unlock?
    
- Would we gain control over our own biology, other people, and even matter itself?
    
- Is the current human state just a primitive form of what we could become?
    

### 2. What is the Fundamental Purpose of Life?

As Lucy's intelligence expands beyond human comprehension, she identifies what she believes is life's primary driving force. The movie proposes a question and a potential answer:

- **Question:** Why are we here? What is the single goal of all life?
    
- **Proposed Answer:** To **pass on knowledge**. The film argues that the ultimate purpose of existence is not just to survive and reproduce, but to preserve and transmit information through time. This frames life itself as a vessel for data.
    

### 3. How Would a Transcendent Being Perceive Reality?

The film explores the concept of "post-human" consciousness.

- Does time exist for a being that can perceive all of reality at once?
    
- Would a transcendent being lose its connection to human concepts like morality, fear, pain, and individuality?
    
- What does reality look like when you can see the connections between all things, from cells to galaxies?
    

### 4. What is Time?

This is the question Professor Norman is obsessed with and the one Lucy ultimately provides an answer for.

- The movie suggests that in the absence of time, there is no meaning, no knowledge, and no life. It proposes that **time is the ultimate organizing principle** that gives existence its structure and allows knowledge to be passed on.
    

### 5. What is the Relationship Between Knowledge and Power?

The film draws a direct line between increasing intelligence and increasing capability.

- It asks if ultimate knowledge would lead to ultimate power—not just political power, but power over physics, time, and space.
    
- It also subtly questions whether such power would make one a god-like benefactor (like Lucy) or a monstrous tyrant (like the criminals seeking the CPH4 drug).
    

In essence, _Lucy_ uses its sci-fi action plot as a vehicle to ask grand, speculative questions about human potential, the nature of reality, and the purpose of existence itself. The entire movie is a "what if" scenario designed to provoke thought on these themes.
